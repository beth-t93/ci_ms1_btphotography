Roots

This project started because of a fascination with history and the local area. The small part of North Wales that was documented in this project was once home to a vast number of factories, most of which helped build the towns and viallges nearby to house their workers.

However this hub of industry has declined over the years, with areas that were once huge factories sitting derelict and nature recaliming the land.

It is this change that was most interesting, and how most people would just see what is now a picturesque path on a part of their dog walk, but upon closer inspection you can find remnants of the past in plain sight.

This project was a piece of work that documented a childhood in present day. Each image represents a part of that childhood, events that happened decades ago, but in present day.

In a way it demonstrates how such simple things can hold such significance to one person, and possibly nothing to the next.

This project stemmed from a love of people watching looking at how people go about their day when out in public.

It then developed into a body of work where all images were taken in portrait, so that once the work was displayed in an exhibtion setting a large number of prints were mounted close together, mimicking the feel you get on a busy highstreet or city centre.

This project was something more personal than the work that had been produced before it. It was a look at family and you begin to associate certain things with different family members, it could be something as simple as a type of light switch or a certain road.
